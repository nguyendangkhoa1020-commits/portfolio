Role

Context

P&G Beauty entered TikTok Shop Vietnam with low GMV while beauty competitors had already invested heavily in livestream and affiliate. As Store Owner through a distributor, my role was to own the total GMV target and the deal list — not to produce content or host shows myself, but to coordinate the key people in charge of each source to reach the shared goal.

Problem

  • Only 1–2 sources were active, so livestream and affiliate power was not used
  • No ROI tracking system per channel — no clear sense of where to put the budget
  • KOL/KOC seeding was based on gut feeling, with no pipeline to sort winners from losers

Action

01
Break down the target by source Split the total GMV target across 5 sources based on expected contribution — the base for weekly tracking and diagnosis.
02
Book and negotiate Live affiliate myself Negotiated directly with KOLs/KOCs, mixing solo booking + MCN network + exclusive booking to keep a stable base of GMV.
03
Track and approve content from the agency Approved concepts for Live inhouse and Video inhouse before production, reviewed performance to guide what comes next.
04
Diagnose and push the right person when target is missed When GMV fell short of target, found which source was weakest, and pushed the right person to take clear action (more bookings, new deals, boost ads with PSA).
05
Turned it into an Operations Map From tracking each source closely, I built a full operations framework — a clear view of Planning/Execution/Optimization/Reporting across all 5 traffic sources.

Result

GMV growth vs baseline +9,300%
Traffic sources tracked 5 / 5
Operations framework Built, transferable
KOL relationships Built myself, tiered

Learning

An owner doesn't need to do everything, just know who to push

The value is not in doing every task yourself — it's in reading the numbers, finding the real problem, and pushing the right person at the right time.

Tracking is the foundation for scaling

Without breaking down the target by source, every push is a guess. Track each source closely from day one.

Direct relationships are hard to copy

The one part I did myself — KOL relationships in Live affiliate — turned out to be the hardest part of the whole system to replace.

Context

A different role from P&G: at Stanhome, I did everything myself — no key person in between. Stanhome is a French pharmacy cosmetics brand with 60 years of history, on Shopee Mall Vietnam but with low GMV — only 20–30 products sold per month. There was no real listing strategy, ads were not optimized, and there was no SKU grouping strategy. I did the SEO listing, set up ads, built an analytics dashboard, and planned the budget across channels myself.

Problem

  • Listings had no main keyword — products did not show up in organic search
  • Ads ran with no SKU data to guide them — budget was spread evenly instead of focused on strong products
  • No system to analyze traffic by channel (Shopee Key, Google, Facebook) to know which one was converting
  • Shop page was not optimized — low CTR, high bounce rate

Action

01
SEO listing for the whole catalog Researched keywords for each SKU, optimized titles to put the main keyword in the first 15 words. Result: 2 key products (moisturizer + body wash) reached the top 10 in Shopee search for their category keyword.
02
Group SKUs by traffic data + conversion rate Built a dashboard to track traffic by source (Shopee Key, Google+Google, Key+Google+Facebook). Sorted into 4 groups: high traffic/high CR → keep as is; high traffic/low CR → optimize ads; low traffic/high CR → raise budget; low traffic/low CR → fix listing + creative.
03
Optimized CPC ads with controlled budget Set up CPC ads for priority SKUs, tracked CTR + ROAS every week. Scaled budget 20% at a time for winning SKUs. Result: shop-wide ROAS reached 9.47 on a 3-month spend.
04
Built a multi-channel budget plan Built a spending plan file by channel (Shopee, Lazada, Facebook, Google, TikTok, Zalo, Tiki) with revenue forecast and cost/revenue ratio per channel — used by management to decide the monthly budget split.
05
Sent regular reports with an optimization loop Sent monthly reports with clear recommendations by SKU group — not just numbers, but the next action to take. In June: Balance Shampoo sales doubled vs May, Soft Shower grew 50%.

Result

Learning

SEO listing is the foundation, not an option

You can turn ads off anytime, but an optimized listing keeps bringing organic traffic forever. Do SEO first, then scale ads — not the other way around.

Group SKUs before spending on ads

Spreading the budget equally across all SKUs is the fastest way to waste money. Group SKUs by traffic × CR to know exactly where to invest.

A report with recommendations beats a report with numbers

A report with only numbers is just a dashboard. The real value is reading the numbers and giving the next action right in the report.
